Line Follower Robot

A Hospitality Robot is basically a robot designed to follow a ‘line’ or path already predetermined by the user. The line or path may be as simple as physical Yellow line on the floor as or complex path marking schemes e.g. embedded lines, magnetic makers or ‘lines’ , vsrious sensing schemes can be employed. These schemes may vary from simple low cost line sensing circuit to expansive vision systems. The choice of these schemes would be dependent upon the sensing accuracy and flexibility required. From the industrial point of view line following robot has been implemented in semi to fully autonomous plants. In this environment, these robots functions as materials carrier to deliver products from one manfacturing point to another where rail, conveyer and gantry solutions are not possible. To control system used must sense the line and man ocurve the robot to stay on course while constatly correcting the wrong moves using feedback mechanism, thus forming a simple yet effective closed-loop system.

To address the drawbacks associated with existing hospitality robots, the proposed system focuses on enhancing versatility, affordability, and reliability. By adopting a modular design approach, the system enables robots to perform multiple tasks within hospitality settings, thereby maximizing their utility and return on investment. Additionally, the implementation of open source software frameworks facilitates seamless integration with existing systems, reducing initial costs and promoting interoperability. To mitigate maintenance concerns, proactive monitoring systems and predictive maintenance algorithms are employed to identify and address potential issues before they escalate, ensuring continuous operation and minimizing downtime. Furthermore, comprehensive training programs and user-friendly interfaces empower hotel staff to effectively interact with and oversee the robots, enhancing their reliability and overall acceptance within the hospitality industry. Through these strategies, the proposed system aims to overcome the limitations of current hospitality robots, providing a scalable, cost-effective, and robust solution for optimizing hotel operations and guest experiences
